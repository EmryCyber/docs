title: "QuickBooks Integration"
description: "Connect each MSP's QuickBooks Online realm for invoice push, payment ledger, and credit memos. Pairs cleanly with Stripe for the dual-system pattern."
---

Each MSP connects their own QuickBooks Online realm to Regentra. The integration lives next to the per-MSP Stripe integration — they're peers, not competitors. Most MSPs run both: QuickBooks for books of record, Stripe for payment processing.

## What this integration does

Pushed automatically when you generate / send / void invoices in Regentra:

| Event | What happens in QuickBooks |
| --- | --- |
| Generate invoice | New `Invoice` created in your QB realm with line items, customer ref, due date |
| Send via QB email | QB emails the customer the invoice from your QB realm (uses BillEmail) |
| Edit DRAFT invoice (notes, due date) | Sparse update to the QB invoice |
| Void invoice | QB invoice voided; if also linked to Stripe, Stripe invoice voided too |
| Mark Paid | QB Payment created applying full balance to the invoice |
| Customer pays via QB | Webhook flips local invoice to PAID; Stripe invoice voided automatically |
| Issue credit memo | New QB CreditMemo against the customer |

Plus continuous sync via Intuit webhooks for incoming payments and invoice status changes — Regentra keeps the local PSA invoice in step with QB without operator intervention.

## Setting up QuickBooks

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get production keys from developer.intuit.com">
    Sign in at [developer.intuit.com](https://developer.intuit.com) → **Dashboard** → your Connector / Regentra app → **Production Settings → Keys and OAuth**. Copy the **Production Client ID** and **Production Client Secret**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the redirect URI on Intuit">
    Same page → **Redirect URIs** → add exactly:

    ```
    https://app.regentra.io/api/settings/integrations/quickbooks/callback
    ```

    Byte-exact match required: HTTPS only, no trailing slash, all lowercase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure webhooks on Intuit">
    Production Settings → **Webhooks**.
    - Notification URL: `https://app.regentra.io/api/webhooks/quickbooks`
    - Subscribe to entities: **Invoice** and **Payment** (skip Customer — Regentra ignores those)
    - Click **Show Verifier Token** and copy it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the env vars in your deployment">
    Set these three in your production deployment env:

    | Env var | Value |
    | --- | --- |
    | `QUICKBOOKS_CLIENT_ID` | Production Client ID |
    | `QUICKBOOKS_CLIENT_SECRET` | Production Client Secret |
    | `INTUIT_WEBHOOK_VERIFIER_TOKEN` | Webhook verifier token |

    Restart the container after saving so Node picks up the new env.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect from Regentra">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations → QuickBooks → Connect QuickBooks**. Sign in with the email that owns your QBO company (NOT your developer.intuit.com account). Authorize Regentra against your realm. You'll land back on the integrations page with the green **Connected** badge.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the mapping">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations → QuickBooks → Configure mapping**. Choose:
    - **Default Service Item** — which QB Service item PSA invoice line items book against (drives your GL account). Optional; falls back to the first active Service item.
    - **Default Income Account** — reference only; QB routes invoice income via the Service item itself.
    - **Online Payment Methods** — see the [QuickBooks Payments section](#quickbooks-payments-pay-now-button) below.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Production keys only work against the live `quickbooks.api.intuit.com` host. **Development keys** target sandbox-quickbooks.api.intuit.com which Regentra does not call. If you paste Development keys, every operation 401s.
</Warning>

<Warning>
  Until your app is approved for **Production** by Intuit (their app-review process, ~1–2 weeks), only the developer's own QB realm can connect. Your customers can't connect their realms until production approval clears.
</Warning>

## QuickBooks Payments (Pay Now button)

Optional. Lets your customers pay through QuickBooks' hosted payment page directly from QB-emailed invoices.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enroll the QB realm in QuickBooks Payments">
    In QuickBooks: **Settings (gear) → Account and Settings → Payments → Set up Payments**. Intuit collects KYC (EIN, deposit account, identity verification) — typically 1–3 business days.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the methods to accept">
    Settings → Integrations → QuickBooks Mapping → **Online Payment Methods**: master Pay Now toggle, credit card, and ACH (US-only). Toggles default ON when QB is your only payment integration, OFF when Stripe is also connected (see [the dual-system section](#using-quickbooks-with-stripe-the-dual-system-pattern) below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push invoices, send via QB email">
    On the Regentra invoice detail page, **Send to QuickBooks** → push the invoice. **Email via QuickBooks** → trigger QB to email the customer with the Pay Now button. Customer clicks → enters card or ACH on Intuit's hosted page → Intuit Payments processes the charge → QB auto-creates a Payment entity → our webhook handler marks the local PSA invoice PAID.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  No card data ever touches Regentra. The Pay Now flow is end-to-end Intuit-hosted — we just set the AllowOnline* flags on the QB invoice body so the button appears.
</Info>

## Using QuickBooks with Stripe (the dual-system pattern)

The most common MSP setup. QuickBooks acts as the books of record (invoices, customers, AR ledger, GL); Stripe acts as the payment processor (collects card / ACH from your customers, deposits net to your bank).

### What Regentra does automatically

When both are connected:

| Customer action | Stripe side | QuickBooks side |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pays via Stripe checkout link | Invoice closes, deposit lands in your bank | Regentra **automatically creates a QB Payment** so the QB ledger reflects the receipt |
| Pays via QB Pay Now (if enabled) | Regentra **automatically voids the linked Stripe invoice** so they can't double-pay | Payment lands, ledger updates |
| Pays you offline (cash, wire) | — | Operator clicks Mark Paid in Regentra → QB Payment created |

The bridges are best-effort and idempotent — webhook redeliveries and double-clicks can't double-book.

### Default behavior protects against duplicate charges

When both Stripe and QB are connected, Regentra defaults the **QB Pay Now toggles to OFF**. The customer sees only the Stripe checkout link. This prevents the parallel-payment-path failure mode where the customer might use both channels and end up double-charged.

You can override this — turn the QB Pay Now toggles back on under **Settings → Integrations → QuickBooks Mapping → Online Payment Methods**. The bridges still keep it safe, but a single payment path is simpler for customers and bookkeeping.

### Stripe processing fee + bank deposit reconciliation

When a customer pays $100 via Stripe, Stripe deposits ~$97 into your bank and keeps ~$3 as a processing fee. Your QB books need three entries to balance:

1. $100 invoice paid (Regentra creates this — the QB Payment).
2. $97 net deposit hitting your bank account.
3. $3 expense recorded against a "Stripe Fees" account.

**Regentra creates entry #1 only.** The other two are out of scope for this integration — they're Stripe-to-QB bookkeeping, not invoice/payment-record sync. For full reconciliation use one of:

| Tool | What it does | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| QBO native Stripe app | Install via QBO → Apps → search Stripe. Pulls deposits + fees into QB. | Free |
| [Acodei](https://www.acodei.com) | Same job, more configuration. | Paid |
| [Synder](https://synder.com/integrations/quickbooks/stripe/) | Same. | Paid |
| [Bookkeep](https://bookkeep.com) | Same. | Paid |

These tools sit alongside Regentra without conflict — they handle the deposit/fee side; Regentra handles the invoice/payment-record side.

## What gets synced (per direction)

### Regentra → QuickBooks

- **Customers**: Portal Companies push to QB Customers via `Sync to QuickBooks` button (or auto-sync on Portal Company edit). Dedupes by primary email so re-syncs don't create duplicates.
- **Invoices**: pushed on `Send to QuickBooks` from the invoice detail page or auto-pushed when "QuickBooks" is checked as a destination on invoice generate.
- **Payments**: created automatically on Stripe webhook + Mark Paid manual action.
- **Voids**: propagated to QB invoice voids.
- **Credit Memos**: created via the **Issue Credit Memo** button on PAID/VOID invoices.

### QuickBooks → Regentra

- **Invoice paid (full)**: webhook flips local invoice to PAID; if linked to Stripe, Stripe invoice auto-voided.
- **Invoice paid (partial)**: webhook flips local invoice to PARTIAL with the remaining balance shown in the UI. Flips to PAID once the rest lands.
- **Payment received**: walks `LinkedTxn[]` to find the affected invoice and reconciles balance.

## Operator kill-switch

If something goes wrong with the QB side and you need to pause all outbound calls without redeploying, set `QUICKBOOKS_ENABLED=false` in your deployment env and restart. The integrations card surfaces a yellow "QuickBooks integration is currently disabled by the operator" banner. Webhook receipts still process (so Intuit doesn't disable our endpoint), but connect, sync, mapping, and invoice push all return 503 with a clear message.

## Troubleshooting

### "QuickBooks integration not configured" on Connect

`QUICKBOOKS_CLIENT_ID` env var is missing in your deployment. Set it (and the other two — `QUICKBOOKS_CLIENT_SECRET`, `INTUIT_WEBHOOK_VERIFIER_TOKEN`) and restart the container.

### "redirect_uri query parameter value is invalid"

The redirect URI you registered on developer.intuit.com doesn't byte-exactly match what Regentra is sending. The string must be:

```
https://app.regentra.io/api/settings/integrations/quickbooks/callback
```

HTTPS only, no trailing slash, all lowercase. Register under **Production** Settings (not Development) — they're separate URI lists.

### "First you'll need to create a company to use with your app"

The Intuit account you signed in with has no QBO company attached. That's almost certainly your developer.intuit.com account, not a real customer account. Sign out of Intuit, sign back in with the email that owns your QBO company, and retry the connect.

### Test Connection succeeded yesterday, fails today

Refresh tokens last ~100 days. If yours expired, the connection-status banner on the QB integration card will say "QuickBooks connection is broken — Reconnect." Click **Reconnect** to re-authorize without losing your mapping configuration.

### Customer paid via Stripe but QB still shows the invoice as outstanding

The auto-bridge runs in the Stripe webhook handler. If it failed (network blip, expired QB token, etc.), the local invoice is PAID but QB ledger isn't. Click **Mark Paid** on the invoice detail page — same `payment-{invoiceId}` idempotency key, so it retries the QB Payment creation. The audit log captures both the failed attempt and the manual retry.

### Stripe invoice still shows "open" after customer paid via QB

Same situation in reverse. The QB-paid → Stripe void bridge is best-effort. Manually void the Stripe invoice from the Stripe dashboard, OR click **Void** on the Regentra invoice detail page (which fires both the local + Stripe + QB voids in one action).

### Realm-already-connected error

Each QuickBooks realm can only be connected to one Regentra organization. If you see "This QuickBooks realm is already connected to another Regentra organization. Disconnect it there before connecting it here." — go to that other org first, disconnect, then retry.

### `intuit_tid` for Intuit support tickets

Every QB API error logs the Intuit-side trace id (`intuit_tid` from the response header). When you contact Intuit support about a failed call, give them this id — it's how their team traces requests on their side. Find the id in your platform logs alongside the `errorClass: QuickBooksApiError` log line.
