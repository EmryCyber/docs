## 6. Data Security

We implement technical and organizational security measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

- **Encryption in transit** via TLS 1.2+ with HSTS enforcement on all connections
- **Encryption at rest** for database storage and AES-256-GCM encryption for integration credentials with unique initialization vectors
- **Multi-factor authentication** (TOTP-based) with enforcement after a grace period
- **Tenant isolation** via application-layer middleware and database row-level security (RLS)
- **Role-based access control** with eight granular permission levels
- **Brute-force protection** with account lockout after five failed authentication attempts
- **Comprehensive audit logging** of all authentication events, data modifications, and administrative actions
- **Automated vulnerability scanning** in our CI/CD pipeline
- **Security headers** including Content Security Policy, X-Frame-Options, X-Content-Type-Options, and Referrer-Policy
- **Input validation and sanitization** on all API endpoints to prevent injection attacks

No method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with our legal and regulatory obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

When determining retention periods, we consider the nature and sensitivity of the information, the purposes for which it is processed, applicable legal requirements, and whether the purposes can be achieved through other means.

- **Account data** is retained for the duration of your active account and for a reasonable period thereafter to allow for reactivation
- **Billing and transaction records** are retained as required by applicable tax and accounting regulations
- **Audit logs** are retained for a minimum period as required by applicable compliance frameworks
- **Session and authentication tokens** expire automatically (sessions: 4–12 hours; refresh tokens: 14 days)
- **Backups** containing personal information are purged on a rolling schedule

Upon termination of a service agreement, Customer Data is deleted within the timeframe specified in the applicable agreement or DPA.

## 9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. We will respond to valid requests within the timeframe required by applicable law (typically 30 days).

### 9.1 Rights Under GDPR (EEA/UK)

If you are located in the EEA or UK, you have the right to:

- **Access** your personal information and receive a copy
- **Rectify** inaccurate or incomplete personal information
- **Erasure** ("right to be forgotten") of your personal information, subject to legal retention obligations
- **Restrict** processing of your personal information
- **Data portability** — receive your information in a structured, machine-readable format
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interest, including direct marketing
- **Withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent, without affecting prior processing

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

### 9.2 Rights Under US State Privacy Laws (CCPA/CPRA and Others)

Residents of California and other US states with comprehensive privacy laws have the right to:

- **Know** what personal information we collect, use, and disclose
- **Access** and receive a copy of your personal information
- **Delete** your personal information, subject to certain exceptions
- **Correct** inaccurate personal information
- **Opt out** of the sale or sharing of personal information

**We do not sell personal information** as defined under the CCPA/CPRA or any other US state privacy law. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

To submit a request, email **privacy@regentra.io**. We will verify your identity before processing your request. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights.

### 9.3 Rights Under Other Jurisdictions

We respect the privacy rights of individuals under applicable laws, including the Canadian Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), the Brazilian Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD), and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). ## 11. Multi-Tenant Architecture and MSP Data

Regentra operates a multi-tenant architecture designed for MSPs managing multiple client organizations:

- **Regentra** acts as a **data processor** (or sub-processor) providing the Platform
- **MSP organizations** act as the **data controller** for their own business data and as a **data processor** for their managed clients' data
- Each client organization's data is logically isolated at both the application and database layers
- MSP administrators may access client data within the scope of their service agreement
- End users accessing the customer support portal can only view data associated with their own organization

The relationship between Regentra, MSPs, and their clients is governed by the applicable service agreement and, where required, a Data Processing Agreement (DPA). To request a DPA, contact **legal@regentra.io**.
